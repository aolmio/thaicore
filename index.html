🎵 The ห Leading Rule
ห before low sonorant (ม น ง ว ล ย ร) elevates it to High Class. ห is silent. Examples: หมา (rising), หนาว (rising).
🔵 The อ Leading Rule
Silent อ before ย/ว elevates them to Mid Class. Example: อยู่ → Low Tone.

🪞 Mid Class Mirror Anchor
Mid class is stable — whatever mark appears, that exact tone sounds: Ek→Low, Tho→Falling, Tri→High, Chattawa→Rising.
🏴‍☠️ Low Class Rebel Shift Jump
Low class + marks jump one level higher: Mai Ek → Falling, Mai Tho → High.
💥 Dead Syllable Choke-Spike Pressure
Low class + short dead → High ˊ. Long dead → Falling ˆ. The stop chokes pitch upward.
🍏 Baseline Checklist
Live: long vowel or nasal ending → Mid baseline.
Dead: stop or short vowel → Low or High by class.
🎵 ห Leading Elevator
ห before sonorant (ม น ง ว ล ย ร) → elevates to High class silently. หมา / หนาว / หวาน → all Rising ˇ.
🔵 อ Leading Silencer
Silent อ before ย/ว → Mid class. อยู่ with Mai Ek → Low Tone.
🕳️ Invisible Vowel
Thai skips /o/ or /a/ between clusters. คน = kh+o+n. กระ = g+r+a.
📈 Pitch Shape Guide
Mid — flat · Low ˋ gentle descent · Falling ˆ peak then sharp drop · High ˊ rises and holds · Rising ˇ dips then climbs.
