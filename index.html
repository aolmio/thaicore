Thai has 44 consonants grouped into Mid (9), High (11), and Low (24) classes — the class determines baseline tone behavior.
● Mid Class — อักษรกลาง (9 consonants)
● High Class — อักษรสูง (11 consonants)
● Low Class — อักษรต่ำ (24 consonants)
Thai has 32 vowel forms. Duration (short vs long) is fundamental — it can flip a syllable from live to dead, changing tone entirely.
● Short Vowels — สระสั้น
● Long Vowels — สระยาว
🟢 Live Syllables — คำเป็น
Ends with a long vowel (no stop), or a sonorant final: -n, -m, -ng, -w, -y. Baseline tone follows normal class rules.
🔴 Dead Syllables — คำตาย
Ends with a short vowel alone, or a plosive stop: -k, -p, -t. Tone is forced into altered paths — no tone mark needed for effect.
🎵 The ห Leading Rule
ห directly before a low class sonorant (ม น ง ว ล ย ร) elevates that consonant to High Class behavior. The ห is silent but acts as a class elevator. Examples: หมา (rising ˇ), หนาว (rising ˇ), หวาน (rising ˇ).
🔵 The อ Leading Rule — อ นำ
Silent อ before ย or ว elevates those low-class consonants to Mid Class. The อ disappears phonetically but controls the class. Examples: อยู่ (yuu) → Low Tone, อย่าง (yaang) → Low Tone.

🪞 The Mid Class Mirror Anchor
Mid class consonants are stable anchors. Whatever tone mark appears, that exact tone sounds perfectly — Ek→Low, Tho→Falling, Tri→High, Chattawa→Rising. No surprises.
🏴‍☠️ The Low Class Rebel Shift Frequency Jump
Low class with tone marks defies expectations — they jump one level higher: Mai Ek (่) → Falling, Mai Tho (้) → High. Think of the rebel climbing above its station.
💥 Dead Syllable Choke-Spike Pressure Rule
Unmarked Low Class + short dead vowel → High Tone ˊ (air pressure spike). Long dead vowel → Falling ˆ. The abrupt stop "chokes" the pitch upward.
🍏 Baseline Syllable Checklist Foundation
Live (คำเป็น): long vowel or nasal ending → Mid Tone baseline.
Dead (คำตาย): stop consonant or short vowel → Low or High depending on class.
🎵 ห Leading Elevator
ห before low class sonorant (ม น ง ว ล ย ร) → acts as High Class. หมา = rising ˇ, หนาว = rising ˇ, หวาน = rising ˇ. ห is silent but it carries the class.
🔵 อ Leading Silencer — อ นำ
Silent อ (mid class) before ย or ว elevates them to Mid Class behavior. อยู่ (yuu) with Mai Ek → Low Tone. The อ is invisible in pronunciation but controls the rule engine.
🕳️ The Invisible Vowel Rule
Thai frequently omits the short inherent vowel /o/ or /a/ between consonants. คน = kh+o+n. กระ = g+r+a. When two consonants appear without a vowel marker, assume a short inherent vowel is hiding between them.
📈 Pitch Contour Memory Aid

Falling ˆ Starts high, curves sharply all the way down.
